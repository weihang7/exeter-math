Format

Individual Rounds

The individual round consists of two parts, taken one after another. The first part has 25 questions in 30 minutes, emphasizing speed. The second part is 8 questions over 40 minutes, emphasizing accuracy in problem solving. Questions in the first part are worth 2 points each, which increases to 5 points each in in the second round. Each student can thus earn up to 90 points, which determines their individual ranking, and is added to the student's team score (so a team can earn up to 360 points from the individual round).

Team Round

The team round is a 45 minute round where everybody on the team can work together to solve 10 questions. The questions will mostly be unrelated to each other, but some of them may be strung together by a common theme. Each question is worth 18 points, so a maximum of 180 points can be earned for the team score in this round.

Guts Round

The Guts Round is a live round consisting of 24 questions in 75 minutes. The questions are given to the teams in sets of three. At the beginning of the round, each team will send a runner down to one of the scoring tables (spaced evenly throughout the contest hall to minimize differences in distance) to get the first set of three problems, and bring it back to their team. The team then works together for as long as they need (within the 75 minutes) to solve these three problems. When they're ready, the runner then drops off their answers at a scoring table, where they will receive the next set of three. At each scoring table, the problems will be scored as soon as they are dropped off, and a running tally of each team's points and progress will displayed on a projector screen at the front of the hall. The point value for each problem increases between each set of three, starting at 4 points per problem in the first set, and ending at 11 points per problem in the last (8th) set, for a total of 180 possible points. The point value for each problem for the eight problem sets are as follows (4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11).

Rules

Prohibited Items

Books, notes, calculators, pocket organizers, slide-rules, abaci, calculator wrist watches, or any other kind of computational aid are prohibited during all parts of the competition. The same goes for graph paper, rulers, protractors, compasses, or any other drawing aid. Similarly, laptops, PDAs, cell phones, or any other electronic communication devices are also not allowed.

Forms of answers

  • Answers need to be simplified and exact unless otherwise specified. (So 3.14 will not work for pi, nor will 4/12 instead of 1/3.)
  • No partial credit will be given unless specified otherwise.
  • Fractions should be simplified and improper. (13/4, not 26/8 or 3 1/4)
  • Radicals should be simplified so that the radicand does not contain any fractions nor be divisible by the square of any integer greater than one. Denominators should be rationalized.
  • Correct mathematical notation should be used.
All decisions made by the EMC2 judges are final.
